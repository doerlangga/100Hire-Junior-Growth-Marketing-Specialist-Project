# Gaetano DiNardi

## Content 1
Date: December 8, 2025
Source URL: https://marketingadvice.substack.com/p/tough-questions-ceos-are-asking-about
Topic: How B2B SaaS marketers should answer CEO questions about AI SEO when organic traffic is flat or declining
Summary: DiNardi publishes a skimmable summary of a 1+ hour in-person interview with Ross Hudgens (Siege Media) on the state of AI SEO in B2B SaaS. He argues the classic rank → CTR → traffic → revenue forecasting model is broken, and that flat organic traffic can actually signal a healthy strategic shift away from decaying top-of-funnel content toward bottom-of-funnel "money pages," comparison content, and "crash-the-party" pages that win in both Google and LLM citations. He warns against over-indexing on ChatGPT conversion-rate screenshots, shows client data where revenue doubled while traffic stayed flat, and introduces the "dark SEO funnel"—users research in LLMs, validate in Google, then convert on the homepage—arguing board decks must track demos, pipeline, deal size, and branded search instead of session volume.
3 Key Takeaways:
- Traffic is not correlated with revenue: when TOFU sections are allowed to decay and BOFU content takes over, flat traffic alongside rising demos and pipeline is a positive signal—not a failure.
- Track commercial LLM prompts, not informational ones: prompts where models must cite external sources (e.g., "best X for Y use case") are where brand visibility matters; "what is X" prompts have zero business value if the model answers from training data alone.
- Build board-ready dark SEO funnel reporting: compare AI vs. Google lead quality (deal size, LTV), show organic channel pipeline growth, and use branded search and homepage traffic as leading indicators when click attribution breaks down.
Why Relevant: DiNardi directly addresses the executive-level skepticism B2B SaaS marketers face when LinkedIn and board conversations turn to "SEO isn't working." His reframing—from traffic obsession to pipeline and dark-funnel attribution—gives organic LinkedIn publishers a credible narrative for defending integrated SEO + founder-led content strategies without chasing vanity traffic metrics.

## Content 2
Date: December 22, 2025
Source URL: https://www.advancedwebranking.com/blog/saas-growth-discovery-in-ai-search-era
Topic: Future of B2B SaaS search across Google, LLMs, and video—podcast interview on The Search Session
Summary: In a 30-minute episode with Gianluca Fiorelli (Advanced Web Ranking), DiNardi discusses how AI is redistributing discovery across Google, ChatGPT, YouTube, and other surfaces. He explains why client demand for SEO advisory remains high despite traffic declines, how the linear CFO attribution model (ranking → clicks → leads) is collapsing under zero-click AI Overviews, and why brand entity salience—not keyword rankings alone—determines LLM recommendations. The conversation covers the dark SEO funnel (LLM research → Google validation → homepage conversion), why B2B SaaS should elevate YouTube and ungated product knowledge from support subdomains, and how product-led, experience-driven content must replace generic TOFU articles that no longer earn clicks or citations.
3 Key Takeaways:
- SEO demand is rising, not dying: DiNardi retained all clients year-over-year because companies still want organic pipeline—but they need advisors to navigate AI-era measurement, not abandon the channel.
- Brand is the new ranking signal for LLMs: appearing in AI answers depends on third-party mentions, review sites, and entity recognition across the web—not just on-page optimization and #1 Google positions.
- Ungate and repurpose proprietary assets: SaaS companies bury product expertise in PDFs and support subdomains; in an LLM-indexed world, that gated knowledge should become searchable, linkable content and video that feeds both SERPs and AI Overviews.
Why Relevant: This interview connects DiNardi's SEO frameworks to the broader organic content ecosystem—including LinkedIn thought leadership, video, and ungated expertise—that B2B SaaS teams need to build entity authority. It validates that LinkedIn organic content is part of a "search everywhere" strategy, not a separate channel from SEO/GEO.

## Content 3
Date: May 7, 2026
Source URL: https://marketingadvice.substack.com/p/the-downfall-of-g2s-seo-has-to-be
Topic: Case-study teardown of G2's SEO decline and what it reveals about the post-helpful-content-update search landscape
Summary: DiNardi analyzes G2's learn.g2 programmatic SEO pages, which lost roughly 852,000 visits (an 85% decline) since their January 2026 peak, tracing the collapse to the September 2023 helpful content update that penalized third-party, SEO-first aggregators. He contrasts G2's failed pivot to "hand-crafted" review pages—first-person language, comparison tables, schema markup, FAQs—that still tank despite checking every tactical SEO box, arguing Google now rewards first-party product owners, task-completion sites, and authentic expertise over ghostwritten "best software" content. He cites Cyrus Shepard's 2026 data on winning-site features (offers a product, proprietary assets, tight topical focus, strong brand) and notes G2's strategic response—acquiring Capterra, Software Advice, and GetApp—while arguing GEO is ultimately a reputation problem, not a tactical one.
3 Key Takeaways:
- Following every SEO best practice is no longer sufficient: G2's "hand-crafted" pages demonstrate that tactical boxes (EEAT signals, schema, first-person copy) fail when content lacks genuine first-hand product experience.
- Google shifted traffic to first-party and community sources: Reddit, YouTube, forums, and brands with proprietary assets win the queries aggregators once owned—challenger SaaS brands must build owned expertise, not mimic review-site playbooks.
- GEO is a reputation and entity problem: paid G2 profiles get cited by LLMs far more than free ones, meaning B2B brands need review presence, analyst coverage, and authentic thought leadership—not just on-page optimization—to influence AI recommendations.
Why Relevant: This teardown is a high-signal cautionary tale for B2B SaaS LinkedIn content strategy: programmatic, generic "best X software" content is dying, while authentic founder and practitioner perspectives gain traction. DiNardi's analysis reinforces why LinkedIn-native, opinionated, experience-based posts outperform SEO-slop—and why organic social content must feed brand entity building that LLMs and Google both reward.
