# Dave Gerhardt

## Content 1
Date: May 5, 2026
Source URL: https://www.exitfive.com/newsletter/pr-is-back-baby
Topic: LinkedIn as the #1 PR channel for B2B founders and marketers
Summary: In Exit Five Newsletter #244, Gerhardt reframes LinkedIn not as a "personal branding" exercise but as a modern PR channel—arguing that B2B teams should build their own media machines instead of waiting for press coverage. Drawing on a Slate Social Social Club panel with Brianna Doe (Verbatim) and Jeff Meltz (Atlassian), he lays out six lessons: treat LinkedIn as PR, not vanity branding; use social as the "third space" where peers find peers; prioritize personal pages over company pages for reach; abandon ghostwritten CEO posts in favor of platform-first, opinionated content; ignore trolls as theater; and start in comments before posting. He connects the thesis to AI search shifts—citing Gartner's projection that 95% of B2B buyer journeys will start in LLMs by 2027—and argues that LinkedIn posts, podcast transcripts, and community threads are shaping what those models recommend. He closes with a compounding argument: after ~10 years of daily writing, he crossed ~199K LinkedIn followers, and consistency—not algorithm hacks—is what builds durable distribution.
3 Key Takeaways:
- Reframe LinkedIn as PR, not personal branding: every post is a blog post, every follower is an opted-in press list, and the CEO's profile is a media channel—not a branding exercise.
- Prioritize people over brand pages: company posts get a fraction of personal-page reach; keep the brand page for news and jobs, but make the CEO, VP Marketing, and other opinionated insiders the real distribution engine.
- Start in comments, not posts: leave thoughtful takes on five peers' posts to find your voice, get on radar with overlapping audiences, and collect prompts for original content—then post twice a week with sharp opinions rather than five times with polished AI slop.
Why Relevant: Gerhardt is one of the most cited voices on B2B LinkedIn organic strategy. This newsletter is his most current articulation of why LinkedIn should be the primary PR channel for B2B SaaS—and gives founders and marketers a practical mental model (PR, not branding) plus a low-friction entry point (comments first) that maps directly to demand-gen and founder-led content programs.

## Content 2
Date: March 6, 2025
Source URL: https://www.exitfive.com/podcast/content-community-and-building-a-200-000-b2b-audience-with-dave
Topic: Building a B2B content flywheel through LinkedIn, podcasts, and community
Summary: On Exit Five Podcast #225 (guest appearance on Siege Media's Content and Conversation), Gerhardt walks through how he turned a decade of organic content into a standalone business. He traces his path from VP Marketing at Drift and CMO at Privy to launching a $10/month Patreon in 2019 that hit 1,000 members in 30 days ($10K MRR), then adding a Facebook group when members asked to talk to each other—eventually rebranding to Exit Five. He explains his content flywheel: test ideas on LinkedIn for real-time engagement signals, then repurpose high-performing posts into newsletters, webinars, podcast episodes, and in-person events (sourcing Drive speakers from top podcast guests). On LinkedIn specifically, he posts once daily between 6–9am, keeps an Apple Notes backlog of post ideas, mines comments on others' posts for future content (e.g., a comment outperforms the original post → becomes a standalone post), and never outsources his LinkedIn writing. He stresses becoming known for one topic (B2B marketing), writing about what you're actually working on rather than forced hot takes, and treating LinkedIn as his primary blog because self-reported attribution shows it as Exit Five's #1 traffic driver.
3 Key Takeaways:
- Run a content flywheel, not a content calendar: use LinkedIn posts as real-time market research—high-engagement topics become newsletters, webinars, podcast episodes, and event programming.
- Post once daily on your core topic and mine comments for ideas: schedule a morning LinkedIn block, keep a backlog of drafts, and turn strong comments on peers' posts into standalone posts when they outperform the original.
- Become known for one thing: 80% of Gerhardt's content is B2B marketing, 20% is personal—niche focus beats posting about everything (fitness, VC, food) because audiences follow you for a specific point of view.
Why Relevant: This episode is Gerhardt's most detailed public breakdown of his daily LinkedIn workflow and the flywheel behind Exit Five's growth. For B2B SaaS teams, it validates LinkedIn as a signal-testing layer that feeds longer-form content—and gives a concrete operating rhythm (daily morning posts, comment mining, no ghostwriting) that scales from founder to small marketing team.

## Content 3
Date: October 23, 2024
Source URL: https://www.reallygood.pro/content/b2b-content/founder-led-content-case-study-exit-five
Topic: Founder-led LinkedIn strategy and the Exit Five content ecosystem
Summary: Really Good Pro's case study documents how Gerhardt bootstrapped Exit Five from a Patreon side hustle (1,000 members at $10/month within six months while CMO at Privy) into a 7-figure community business by treating his LinkedIn presence as the primary growth engine. Per Exit Five COO Matthew Carnevale, 60.4% of new member signups come from LinkedIn. The strategy rests on three pillars: Dave's personal brand (~173K followers, almost-daily posts that drove 1M+ impressions for the Drive event without paid press), a media arm repurposing 170+ podcast episodes and 20+ webinars across the company page and employee accounts, and "building influencers from within"—team members (Matthew at 8K, Danielle at 4K, Dan Murphy at 18K) posting in coordinated bursts that recently drove 35 new trials and ~$6,600 in annual revenue with no UTMs or paid promotion. The piece also maps Exit Five's full content ecosystem (LinkedIn, podcast 2x/week, 20K+ newsletter subscribers, YouTube, events) and notes sponsorship revenue eventually outpaced community revenue as the brand became a media business.
3 Key Takeaways:
- Founder-led LinkedIn is the top-of-funnel engine: ~60% of Exit Five signups trace to LinkedIn, and a single founder post about an in-person event generated 1M+ impressions without journalists or ad spend.
- Multiply reach through employee advocacy, not just the founder: coordinate team posts over several days to drive trials and revenue—each employee's POV adds trust and reaches audiences who may not relate to a 7-figure founder.
- Build a repurposing ecosystem, not isolated posts: podcast episodes, webinars, and newsletter content feed LinkedIn clips and takeaways across founder and company pages so the team never runs out of native, value-first content to distribute.
Why Relevant: This third-party case study provides hard metrics (60.4% LinkedIn attribution, 1M+ event impressions, employee-post trial results) behind Gerhardt's LinkedIn playbook. For B2B SaaS teams evaluating founder-led or employee-advocacy programs, it shows how one consistent founder voice plus a small team of posting employees can replace paid acquisition for community and pipeline growth.
