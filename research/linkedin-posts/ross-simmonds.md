# Ross Simmonds

## Content 1
Date: January 14, 2024
Source URL: https://rosssimmonds.com/blog/content-distribution-guide/
Topic: The Ultimate Guide to Content Distribution — owned/earned/paid channels and a repeatable distribution playbook
Summary: Simmonds argues that most marketing teams over-invest in content creation and under-invest in distribution. He defines content distribution as the strategic dissemination of content across owned (website, blog, email), earned (PR, social mentions, dark social, organic SEO), and paid channels. He maps the full content marketing engine as Research → Creation → Distribution → Optimization, with distribution spanning email, SEO, social (including LinkedIn as the go-to B2B platform), video, podcasts, influencers, sales, and communities. The post includes a post-publish playbook across six channels and a three-phase distribution workflow: Distribution Preparation, Publish Week amplification, and Content Repurposing—plus best practices like tailoring content per channel, measuring cross-channel results, and using hashtags strategically on LinkedIn.
3 Key Takeaways:
- Treat distribution as equal to creation: the best content engines run on four pillars—research, creation, distribution, and optimization—and teams that skip distribution leave reach and ROI on the table.
- Match channels to audience behavior before posting: for B2B SaaS, LinkedIn is the default professional channel, but channel selection should be driven by where your ICP actually spends time, whether they engage there, and whether the channel can drive measurable ROI.
- Run a repeatable three-phase playbook after every publish: prepare assets for amplification, execute a dedicated publish-week push across email/SEO/social/sales, then repurpose top performers into new formats (carousels, threads, video clips, email drips) to extend shelf life without net-new creation.
Why Relevant: Simmonds is the founder of Foundation Marketing (B2B SaaS content agency) and author of *Create Once, Distribute Forever*. This guide is the foundational framework behind his LinkedIn-native message—"put marketing back into content marketing"—and gives B2B SaaS teams a channel-by-channel playbook for turning one insight into multi-platform organic reach, with LinkedIn explicitly positioned as the primary B2B distribution surface.

Source URL: https://www.linkedin.com/posts/rosssimmonds_marketing-activity-7312130237562044417-j-Qj
Topic: "Create once → Distribute forever" LinkedIn post on repurposing podcast content into multi-channel assets
Summary: In this LinkedIn post, Simmonds challenges marketers who keep producing more blog posts, ebooks, and podcasts without a distribution plan. He restates his core motto—Create once → Distribute forever—and lists concrete repurposing outputs from a single podcast episode: keyword-targeted blog posts, social posts for X/Mastodon/Bluesky, sales-enablement slide decks, short-form video scripts, LinkedIn carousels, and email campaigns. He acknowledges the time constraint ("I HAVE NO TIME") and points readers toward Distribution.ai, the repurposing tool his team built to automate the workflow his book describes.
3 Key Takeaways:
- One source asset should spawn six-plus derivative formats: a single podcast can become SEO blog posts, social threads, sales decks, video scripts, LinkedIn carousels, and email campaigns—maximizing ROI from content teams already producing long-form assets.
- LinkedIn carousels are a first-class distribution output, not an afterthought: Simmonds explicitly lists carousels alongside blog posts and email as standard repurposing targets, signaling that slide-deck-style posts are core to his organic LinkedIn playbook.
- Distribution is the bottleneck, not creation: the post's opening line ("You don't need more blog posts, ebooks or podcasts—you need better distribution") reframes the B2B SaaS content problem from volume to systematic redistribution after publish.
Why Relevant: This is a native LinkedIn post from a B2B SaaS content agency CEO with 59K+ followers, demonstrating the exact repurposing workflow B2B SaaS marketers need for organic LinkedIn: turn one high-effort asset (podcast, webinar, case study) into a week of platform-native LinkedIn content without starting from scratch each time.

## Content 3
Date: February 26, 2026
Source URL: https://rosssimmonds.com/blog/reddit-dark-funnel-software/
Topic: How Reddit became the dark funnel for enterprise software—and what B2B vendors should do about it
Summary: Simmonds makes the case that enterprise software deals are increasingly decided in Reddit threads months before any CRM touchpoint. He cites data showing Reddit's 1,328% search-visibility surge (mid-2023 to mid-2024), 72% of tech decision-makers using Reddit for peer reviews (Forrester 2025), 70% of the B2B journey happening anonymously (6sense), and Reddit as the #1 cited domain in Google AI Overviews and Perplexity for software queries. He explains why attribution fails (anonymous browsing, stripped UTMs, indirect conversion paths) and recommends monitoring subreddits for 90 days before marketing, adding a free-text "How did you hear about us?" field to demo forms, accepting that product quality is the best Reddit "marketing," and recognizing that Reddit reputation now shapes AI search answers (GEO/AEO).
3 Key Takeaways:
- 68% of Reddit users aren't on LinkedIn—B2B SaaS teams relying solely on LinkedIn organic miss a large slice of anonymous buyers researching software in subreddits like r/sysadmin, r/devops, and r/SaaS before ever raising their hand.
- Attribution models systematically undercount Reddit and dark social: buyers read peer threads, then search the vendor directly or ask an LLM (which cites Reddit), so last-click attribution credits "organic search" or "direct" instead of the thread that shaped the shortlist.
- Reddit reputation and AI search reputation are converging: with Reddit as the top citation source in AI Overviews and Perplexity for software queries, positive peer sentiment on Reddit directly influences how ChatGPT and Perplexity recommend vendors—making off-LinkedIn distribution a complement to LinkedIn organic strategy.
Why Relevant: For B2B SaaS teams building LinkedIn organic pipelines, Simmonds shows where LinkedIn fits in a broader organic distribution stack. His dark-funnel framework explains why LinkedIn content alone won't capture the full buyer journey—and gives actionable tactics (self-reported attribution, subreddit monitoring, GEO-aware content) to align LinkedIn publishing with the channels that actually shape enterprise software shortlists.
