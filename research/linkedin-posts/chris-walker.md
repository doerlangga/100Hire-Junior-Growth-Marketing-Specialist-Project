# Chris Walker

## Content 1
Date: October 23, 2023
Source URL: https://www.linkedin.com/posts/chriswalker171_demand-marketing-b2b-activity-7122201912904486912-jj3T
Topic: Measuring LinkedIn ROI with self-reported attribution vs. multi-touch attribution
Summary: Walker published one of his most-cited LinkedIn posts comparing how Refine Labs measures LinkedIn performance. Using self-reported attribution ("How did you hear about us?") since July 2021, Refine Labs attributed $50MM in HIRO pipeline and $14MM in net-new closed-won ARR to LinkedIn over two years. The same activity measured through multi-touch attribution software showed only $977K in closed-won revenue—a 93% gap. He argues B2B marketers still evaluate content the way they did in 2013 (tracked desktop form fills) even though content formats, distribution, privacy policies, and social platforms have fundamentally changed. His prescription: stop proving ROI of individual content pieces via touchpoint attribution; instead measure demand-creation channels holistically through customer-reported data (forms, sales-call tags, ICP surveys, win/loss interviews). Attribution software remains useful for demand capture, but not for the full marketing mix.
3 Key Takeaways:
- Multi-touch attribution dramatically underreports LinkedIn and dark social: Refine Labs' own data showed a 93% measurement gap ($14MM vs. $977K) for the same LinkedIn activity—explaining why most B2B teams underinvest in organic LinkedIn despite strong real-world returns.
- Measure channel-level ROI through accumulation, not per-post attribution: demand creation results come from sustained content volume and touchpoints over time; most never register in digital attribution tools.
- Implement self-reported attribution as the primary demand-creation measurement layer: automate "How did you hear about us?" in Salesforce/MAP, tag first-call answers in conversation intelligence, survey ICP buyers not in-market, and run win/loss interviews—then combine with software data in a hybrid model.
Why Relevant: This is Walker's signature LinkedIn post and the data point that made his organic-content thesis go viral across B2B marketing. For SaaS teams building founder-led or employee LinkedIn programs, it provides a concrete business case for investing in native LinkedIn content even when attribution software shows near-zero pipeline—and a practical measurement alternative executives can accept.

## Content 2
Date: April 7, 2023
Source URL: https://www.refinelabs.com/article/hybrid-attribution-framework
Topic: The Attribution Mirage and Refine Labs' Hybrid Attribution Framework for dark social
Summary: Refine Labs published a twelve-month data study analyzing 620 declared-intent conversions and $21.5MM in closed-won ARR, comparing software-based attribution against self-reported customer data. The study found a 90% measurement gap—what Walker calls "The Attribution Mirage"—where software over-relies on trackable digital touches and fails to capture demand-creation programs in dark social (social media, podcasts, word of mouth, communities). A striking example: podcasts were attributed to 53% of revenue ($11.4MM) via self-reported data but 0% via software attribution. In response, Refine Labs released its Hybrid Attribution Framework, combining software data (for demand capture) with first-party customer insights (for demand creation). Walker, then Chairman of Refine Labs, framed it as a milestone for modernizing demand gen and helping revenue leaders make informed GTM investment decisions.
3 Key Takeaways:
- Dark social channels are systematically invisible to attribution software: social media, podcasts, communities, and word-of-mouth are drastically under-reported or entirely missed—creating a false picture that overweights SEO, direct traffic, and paid search.
- Use hybrid attribution, not software-only models: pair digital attribution (last-touch or multi-touch) for demand capture with self-reported attribution on high-intent forms to surface what customers actually say drove their purchase decision.
- Demand-creation ROI requires first-party customer data: when the most impactful programs can't be tracked digitally, revenue teams must ask buyers directly and synthesize qualitative insights with quantitative software inputs.
Why Relevant: This is the research foundation behind Walker's entire LinkedIn content philosophy. B2B SaaS marketers posting organically on LinkedIn operate in a "dark social" channel that attribution tools cannot see—this study gives them the data and framework to justify LinkedIn organic investment to leadership and reallocate budget away from over-measured capture channels.

## Content 3
Date: April 4, 2023
Source URL: https://openviewpartners.com/blog/find-a-new-hybrid-attribution-model-for-your-enterprise-demand/
Topic: Enterprise demand creation, broken attribution, and why gated content and lead gen fail modern B2B buyers
Summary: In an OpenView Partners interview, Walker explains why enterprise marketing is broken: buyers now discover products through peers, podcasts, LinkedIn posts, webinar chats, and communities—but attribution software tags them as organic search or direct traffic. He shares Refine Labs' year-long study of $21.5MM in closed-won revenue: HubSpot attribution credited 80% of revenue to organic search, direct, and paid search, while customers self-reported only 3% from those channels. Conversely, customers attributed 41% to social media and 53% to podcasts; software showed 3% and 0% respectively. Walker advocates a hybrid attribution model, ungated content distributed natively on LinkedIn and podcasts, and demand-creation over lead-generation tactics. He also outlines buyer-intent segmentation (declared, assumed, low/no intent) and signals that founders "get" marketing—chief among them, whether the CEO posts actively on LinkedIn.
3 Key Takeaways:
- Buyers research on LinkedIn and dark social, but attribution credits direct/organic search: this misalignment causes companies to over-invest in capture channels (SEO, paid search, gated eBooks) and under-invest in the LinkedIn content buyers actually cite.
- Ungated, platform-native content changes your entire content strategy: removing gates shifts mediums beyond PDFs, changes what you create (value over headline bait), and enables redistribution on LinkedIn, podcasts, and live events—the channels that create demand.
- Founder LinkedIn activity is a leading signal of GTM sophistication: CEOs who post frequently on LinkedIn recognize dark social's impact even when attribution doesn't measure it; companies demanding "5,000 leads" or full software attribution are negative signals.
Why Relevant: Walker's OpenView interview connects his attribution thesis directly to LinkedIn organic strategy for B2B SaaS—ungated native content, founder-led posting, and peer-trust-driven discovery. It gives SaaS teams a executive-ready narrative for shifting from MQL/lead-gen LinkedIn tactics to demand-creation content that builds pipeline attribution software will never capture.
