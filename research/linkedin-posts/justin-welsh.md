# Justin Welsh

## Content 1
Date: June 7, 2025
Source URL: https://justinwelsh.me/essays/my-10m-journey
Topic: Complete 23-step journey to $10M in solopreneur revenue through organic content
Summary: Welsh documents how he reached $10M in total revenue over five years and nine months using 100% organic social posting on LinkedIn, X/Twitter, and Instagram, plus newsletter and SEO—no paid ads. He walks through 23 sequential steps, from posting daily on LinkedIn as a former SaaS CRO to find his voice, through building a consulting practice, launching digital products (The LinkedIn Operating System, The Content Operating System, The Creator MBA), and scaling via affiliates, newsletters, and sponsorships. The essay emphasizes listening to customer signals, aligning content with products, and maintaining ~89% profit margins as a one-person business.
3 Key Takeaways:
- Start with high-volume content to find signal: post consistently about what you know, track engagement (not vanity metrics), then double down on what resonates and systematize it into a daily LinkedIn workflow.
- Align content and product tightly before scaling: when 100% of LinkedIn content focused on audience growth and matched his LinkedIn course, sales accelerated from $75K in 15 months to $186K in 3 months.
- Follow customer-paid signals, not your original niche: DMs revealed demand for LinkedIn growth advice over SaaS consulting, leading to his first $10K/month digital product and eventual flagship courses.
Why Relevant: Welsh is a former B2B SaaS executive who built an 800K+ LinkedIn following and $12M+ in revenue through organic posting alone. His LinkedIn Operating System—volume → signal → system → product alignment—is a directly transferable playbook for B2B SaaS teams building founder-led or company LinkedIn demand gen without paid media.

## Content 2
Date: September 20, 2025
Source URL: https://www.justinwelsh.me/newsletter/leverage
Topic: Five leverage points for solopreneur growth, with emphasis on content multiplication and distribution
Summary: In this Saturday Solopreneur newsletter issue, Welsh argues that solopreneur scale comes from pulling specific leverage points—not adding complexity. He details five: the content multiplication effect (5-12-3 rule), time arbitrage through delegation, "hell yes" filtering for partnerships, story-driven selling over feature lists, and distribution obsession. He shares his current ~12M monthly impression breakdown, with LinkedIn driving 7M impressions (~58%), and explains his weekly repurposing workflow from X posts to LinkedIn, Instagram, Threads, and Substack.
3 Key Takeaways:
- Apply the 5-12-3 rule to every piece of content: hook in 5 seconds, aim for 12-month evergreen relevance, and design each idea to work across at least three platforms before publishing once.
- Go all-in on one distribution channel first: Welsh spent two years focused exclusively on LinkedIn before expanding, because spreading too thin early makes creators invisible everywhere.
- Lead with story, not features: The Creator MBA sold at 5x his other courses because the sales narrative created recognition and empathy before listing modules—story determines whether prospects stay to discover product quality.
Why Relevant: The 5-12-3 repurposing framework and channel-first distribution strategy give B2B SaaS marketers a repeatable system for turning one insight into a week of LinkedIn-native content. His distribution breakdown validates LinkedIn as the primary organic channel for reaching professional buyers—a core assumption for B2B SaaS LinkedIn strategy.

## Content 3
Date: January 1, 2025
Source URL: https://www.linkedin.com/posts/justinwelsh_2024-as-a-solopreneur-2xed-revenue-activity-7280203109975425025-hfOM
Topic: 2024 year-in-review LinkedIn post blending business metrics with personal lessons
Summary: Welsh published his annual solopreneur recap on LinkedIn, reporting 2x revenue growth, ~86% margins, two new product launches, 177K+ new LinkedIn followers, 43K+ new X followers, and 700+ five-star testimonials. Beyond metrics, he shared six lessons from 2024: simplify everything, stay positive amid negativity, underpromise and overdeliver, disconnect and go offline, build life before business, and recognize most people share common goals despite different views. He highlighted customer referrals (1,307 product sales) and personal wins like 50+ lunches with his wife and a 90-day move abroad.
3 Key Takeaways:
- Simplification drives performance: Welsh found that adding complexity hurt results, while zooming out and stripping back overly complex offers and workflows made everything work better.
- Customer understanding beats hype: by selling through deep customer empathy rather than hype, his customers generated 1,307 referral sales in 2024—organic word-of-mouth as a primary growth lever on LinkedIn.
- LinkedIn recap posts as trust-building content: blending transparent business metrics (margins, follower growth, testimonials) with personal values creates high-engagement, shareable posts that reinforce credibility without a hard sell—ideal for B2B founder brand content.
Why Relevant: This post demonstrates a proven LinkedIn organic format for B2B operators: transparent results + actionable lessons + personal authenticity. For B2B SaaS founders and marketers, the recap structure shows how to convert a year of work into a single high-trust post that builds audience, social proof, and inbound demand without paid promotion.
